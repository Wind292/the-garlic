About The Garlic

Our Mission
"To report the news with the accuracy we can manage and the conviction we cannot always justify."
Who We Are

The Garlic is an independent newspaper founded in 1967 by Edmund Pryce, a man who believed strongly in the public's right to be informed and only slightly less strongly in their ability to handle it.

We are owned by no corporation, beholden to no political party, and answerable to no advertiser — though we remain, as always, politely grateful to those who advertise with us and would prefer they continue.

Our newsroom employs journalists across four continents, two of whom have won awards. We are proud of all of them equally, but particularly those two.

Our History
Nearly Six Decades of Getting It Mostly Right
  • 1967 The Garlic is founded by Edmund Pryce in a rented office above a dry cleaner in the city centre. First issue sells 340 copies. Pryce considers this a triumph.
  • 1974 Circulation reaches 10,000. The dry cleaner below closes. Pryce has always maintained this was a coincidence.
  • 1989 The Garlic wins its first journalism award. Staff celebrate. The award is later found to have been issued by an organisation Pryce himself founded. The celebration is not rescinded.
  • 1998 The paper launches its first website. It is, by the standards of 1998, quite good.
  • 2008 Edmund Pryce retires. His farewell editorial runs to 6,000 words. The paper prints all of it.
  • 2011 Cornelius Ashby appointed Editor-in-Chief. Circumstances remain undisclosed.
  • 2019 Digital subscriptions exceed print for the first time. The news desk marks the occasion with a cake that said "print is dead" and then felt bad about it.
  • 2026 The Garlic continues. We are as surprised as anyone.
The Newsroom
The People Behind The Paper
Leadership
Cornelius Ashby
Editor-in-Chief
Cornelius has led The Garlic since 2011, when he was appointed under circumstances he declines to discuss. He previously served as deputy editor for nine years and, before that, as a man who sent a great many strongly-worded letters to other newspapers.
Helena Marsh
Managing Editor
Helena manages the day-to-day operations of the newsroom with a calm that colleagues describe as either admirable or deeply suspicious. She joined The Garlic in 2014 from a regional paper that no longer exists, for unrelated reasons.
Bertram Osei
Publisher
Bertram oversees the business side of The Garlic, including advertising, subscriptions, and the annual Gala. He has strong opinions about the price of paper and shares them freely.
Editorial
Margaret Holloway
Senior Correspondent, World Affairs
Margaret has reported from 34 countries, most of them willingly. She speaks four languages and reads three of them.
Thomas Aldridge
Economics Editor
Thomas joined the paper in 2009 and has been covering central bank policy ever since. He has not visibly enjoyed any of it, but his work is considered authoritative.
Priya Nair
Technology Correspondent
Priya covers Silicon Valley from our San Francisco bureau. She was named one of the top technology journalists of 2024 by a publication she has asked us not to name.
Eleanor Voss
Contributing Editor, Opinion
Eleanors column runs every other Thursday and generates more correspondence than any other feature in the paper. Not all of it is hostile.
Operations
Douglas Fenn
Head of Design
Douglas has designed The Garlics print edition since 2003. He has opinions about typefaces that he will share if asked, and occasionally if not.
Imogen Clarke
Head of Digital
Imogen joined in 2018 to oversee the papers digital transition. She describes the process as "ongoing." We feel this is accurate.
